project 2: hypernarrative

my hypernarrative is about the many songs i have written. i always wanted to share them, but there were so many things that impeded me too. one is that i never really shared my passion for music, especially now that i've been in MAP where i focus on other fields, but the main reason is because none of these songs are actually finished. because things weren't finished, i felt that they would be criticized more than if they were done. this perfectionistic view caused me to not share nor finish them at all. however, i've come to realize that unfinished things can be appreciated. these songs highlight a different time in my life, and it illustrates my growth now. this project also taught me to not focus so much on finishing things or constantly reaching a goal. i am also an unfinished being, and i still can enjoy many parts of life such as my music!

project 3: networked justice

My concept for Project 3 will center around my documentary series I created last semester over the novel The House On Mango Street by Sandra Cisneros. The major theme revolving around the novel and the documentary is the diverse Hispanic immigrant experience. Through each episode, viewers are introduced to a Hispanic individual from Wichita, KS, and each story is a unique glimpse into one’s journey as a Hispanic immigrant or child of immigrants. For unknown reasons, Big Read was not able to showcase the series, so this website is for all those who were a part of the process so that they can see the end product of the series. Additionally, it allows those to learn more about the people in my community. They’ve made such big differences in the city, and their experiences should be highlighted. It allows everyone to reevaluate concerns around immigration and being a person of color in a predominantly white town.
